Arithumpally

Athirappilly is a picturesque village located in the Thrissur district of Kerala, India. Renowned for its stunning waterfalls and lush greenery, Athirappilly is often referred to as the "Niagara of India" due to its striking resemblance to the famous Niagara Falls in North America. The main attraction in Athirappilly is the Athirappilly Waterfalls, which cascades down from a height of approximately 80 feet, creating a breathtaking spectacle of gushing water amidst the verdant forests of the Western Ghats. The roaring sound of the waterfalls and the misty spray that envelops the surrounding area add to the enchanting ambiance, making it a popular spot for tourists and nature enthusiasts alike. Apart from the Athirappilly Waterfalls, the village is also home to the Vazhachal Waterfalls, located a short distance downstream. With its scenic beauty and tranquil surroundings, Vazhachal Waterfalls offers visitors another opportunity to immerse themselves in the natural splendor of the region. Athirappilly is situated near the Sholayar Range of the Western Ghats, making it an ideal destination for trekking and hiking enthusiasts. The lush forests and pristine riverside trails provide ample opportunities for exploration and adventure amidst the breathtaking landscapes of Kerala. The village is also home to the Athirappilly Hydroelectric Project, which harnesses the power of the waterfalls to generate electricity for the region. Visitors can learn about the engineering marvels behind the project and witness the impressive sight of water being diverted for power generation. Accommodation options in Athirappilly range from luxury resorts and eco-friendly lodges to budget guesthouses, ensuring that visitors can find a comfortable stay amidst the natural beauty of the region. Overall, Athirappilly is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers, offering a perfect blend of stunning landscapes, cultural richness, and tranquil ambiance that make it a must-visit destination in Kerala.
